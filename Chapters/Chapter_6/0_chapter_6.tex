\chapter{Conclusion and future works}
In this thesis, we presented a multimodal quality inspection framework for the food industry, specifically designed for the bread production process. The framework integrates various techniques, including motion detection, DEIMv2 training, volume calculation with 3 different and comparted methods for segmentations and 3 other different methods for volume calculation, Anomaly detection and anomaly map and score evaluation,to assess the quality of dough balls and bread products. The implementation of the framework was carried talking a lot with the operators of the SMACT \cite{smact_official_2026} plant, ensuring that the developed solution is tailored to the specific needs and constraints of the production environment and planning and engineering the solutions.
The results obtained from the framework showed promising outcomes in terms of dough-ball volume estimation and bread quality assessment.For the first evaluation we can say for sure that convex hull is the best method used and SAM for segmentation would have been perfect, but for computation efficiency, since it is easy in this case, I chose a cluster+bgs approach that is valid . For the second one, the anomaly score on the good dataset is amazingly good, almost perfect, while, even though the bad bread distribution wasn't even in the dataset that we pick up distribution, the results were really great and the anomaly maps correctly assign the anomalies where they are.
The quantitative and qualitative results for both Site 1 and Site 2 indicated that the framework is effective in monitoring the production process and identifying potential quality issues.
Future works could focus on further improving the accuracy and efficiency of the framework, maybe by measuring more GT data for volume assessment or grasping some effective bad results to add to defect dataset and train the anomaly net to. as well as the development of real-time monitoring capabilities to provide immediate feedback during the production process . Additionally, because of the modularity of the code, expanding the framework to other food products and industries could further demonstrate its versatility and applicability in various manufacturing contexts.
